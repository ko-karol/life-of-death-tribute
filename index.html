The Life of Death

A tribute to Marsha Onderstijn

Scroll

"Because I could not stop for Death – He kindly stopped for me"

Emily Dickinson

The Encounter

A small thing, trembling and new.

Death kneels. Not to take — but to see.

It reaches out, and the flowers blacken at its touch.

Yet it withdraws. Not yet.

The Waiting

The fawn grows. Learns to run.

Death watches from the treeline.

It has nowhere else to be.

Seasons pass. The antlers grow. Death remains.

"You would know the secret of death. But how shall you find it unless you seek it in the heart of life?"

Kahlil Gibran

Always Near

At the edge of the meadow.

Behind the last tree before the clearing.

Never chasing. Only present.

The deer does not run. Perhaps it knows.

The Embrace

The deer lies down in the snow.

Death finally crosses the clearing.

It kneels once more. This time, it stays.

A hand on weathered fur. The long exhale.

Death was never the enemy.

It was the one who waited longest to say goodbye.

"For what is it to die but to stand naked in the wind and to melt into the sun?"

Kahlil Gibran
