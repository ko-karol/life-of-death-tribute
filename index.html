The Life of Death

A tribute to Marsha Onderstijn's meditation on mortality

Scroll to begin

The Encounter

Death does not hunt.

It simply arrives — quiet, curious, patient.

"From the moment we are born, death walks beside us. Not as enemy, but as companion."

The Waiting

Through seasons turning.

Through moments of joy and sorrow.

Death waits — never rushing, never impatient.

"Time means nothing to death. It has witnessed every spring and will witness every winter yet to come."

Always Near

In the rustle of autumn leaves.

In the stillness of a winter morning.

In the shadow at the edge of every joyful gathering.

"We spend our lives running from what has always been beside us."

The Embrace

And when the time comes —

not a taking, but a receiving.

Not an ending, but a return.

"Death's embrace is not cold. It is the final warmth after a long journey."

Perhaps death is not the opposite of life,

but the proof that life was lived.
